Hello, welcome to
pain learning research

Here you will find a collection of online games and questionnaires that help us understand how your pain changes in time. There are also useful links on pain management.

Who are we?

We are a group of neuroscientists and clinicians based at the University of Cambriddge and University of Oxford. We want to understand the role of learning in chronic pain development.

Pain teaches us how to protect our body. Although this is beneficial in normal circumstances, an "overactive" pain system could amplify pain beyond what is necessary. Learning processes gone wrong might be central to the transition to, or maintenance of, chronic pain.

You can help us understand how pain changes the learning process by tracking your thinking and movement using our online tools.

Online Games

Pop a balloon

Pop a balloon to find out if you've won a coin or poked a naughty pirate! It helps us evaluate your decision to reward and loss (10 mins, if asked for an ID, please put the number 1 in).

Road safety

Rocks are falling on your car, you need to steer clear by watching the road signs. Should you use your shield when you see a star or a circle? It evaluates how loss may change your perception of safety (10 mins, if asked for an ID, please put the number 1 in).

Moving circles

Touch the correct circle as fast as you can to win points! Testing how fast your movements are help us gauge your motor learning (10 mins, if asked for an ID, please put the number 1 in).

Stocks forecast

What's your prediction of the stock market? Should you sell or hold? How sure are you about this? It'll tell us about how you change your mind based on earlier observations (1 hour, if asked for an ID, please put the number 1 in).

Symptom tracking

Visual analog scale (VAS)
How are you feeling today? Rate your pain, fatigue and mood (4 questions)
Brief pain inventory (BPI)
Rapidly assesses the severity of pain and its impact on functioning (13 questions)
Short-Form McGill (SF-MPQ)
Measures the sensory and affective dimension of pain (17 questions)
Fatigue scale (FACIT)
Measures your general fatigue levels (13 questions)
Beck depression inventory (BDI)
Measures characteristic attitudes and symptoms of depression (21 questions)
BIS/BAS scale
Assesses individual differences in the sensitivity to appetitive and aversive motives (13 questions)
